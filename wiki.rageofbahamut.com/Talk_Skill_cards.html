[2012/07/11] Ah yeah, I was being led astray by my own assumption that skill bonuses stack, thanks for confirming that they don't. That made all the pieces fall into place. Funnily enough, the animation is actually a faithful indication of how things stack. For illustration, I'll present the case I've been studying all this time: 

Valkyrie Hild ATK = 13982 Great Boost to Gods ATK (15%)
Brunilde++ ATK = 12752 Big Boost to All ATK lvl3 (10%)
Total ATK = 26734
Actual damage dealt after both procs = 36432

So the sequence goes like this: 

Valk's "turn": 
She gets +5% ATK from order bonus and +5% from realm bonus = 13982 + 10% = 13982 + 1398.2 = 15380.2. Round up to 15381 (new "base value")
Her ability procs = 15381 + 15% = 15381 + 2307.15 = 17688.15. Round up to 17689.

Brun's "turn":
She gets +5% ATK from order bonus and +5% from realm bonus = 12752 + 10% = 12752 + 1275.2 = 14027.2. Round up to 14028 (new "base value")
Her ability procs = 14028 + 10% = 14028 + 1402.8 = 15430.8. Round up to 15431.

Here's where the animation hinted me what was going on behind curtains: Valk's proc triggers before Brun's numbers are calculated and Brun's proc triggers after Valk's numbers have been calculated. How does the computer apply their bonuses to each other, then? Here's the twist: it applies them to their corresponding "real" base values, not the ones including order and realm bonuses. So: 

Valk's ATK becomes 17689 + (13982 x 0.10) = 17689 + 1398.2 = 19087.2. Round up again to 19088.
Brun's ATK becomes 15431 + (12752 x 0.15) = 15431 + 1912.8 = 17343.8. Round up again to 17344.

Total ATK = 19088 + 17344 = 36432. Success!!

Interestingly, the "mystery bonus" doesn't appear anywhere in this formula. --Pylgrim

Raid Boss Damage Skills -- Lucasmembrane (talk) 17:13, 21 June 2014 (EDT) -- Lucasmembrane (talk) 17:13, 21 June 2014 (EDT)&lt;editsection-brackets&gt;n">[edit]&lt;editsection-brackets&gt;

Methodology: Ensure that raid boss animations are turned on. Attack a raid boss with 1 AP using a 1-card event deck, with the appropriate skill card in the leader position of group 1 and no other cards. Depending on the card's HP, it will get 1-5 hits on the boss before dying or ending in a draw. Record the damage for each hit, as well as whether or not the skill activated. Also record whether or not rising break was active, whether or not atk boost activated, and the combo multiplier (when applicable). For each hit, divide by the combo multiplier, 1.2 for atk boost, and 2 for rising break; the result is the base damage with/without skill. If the skill hits too often to get a good sample of no-skill hits, put the card into position 2 of the event deck, with nothing in the leader position.

With a sufficiently large number of trials, you'll observe that there are six distinct values for damage without skill and six with skill. When these two groups are ranked, the ratios of each pair (ie, lowest with skill to lowest without skill, #2 with to #2 without, etc.) will be the same - this is the damage multiplier for that boss, skill, and skill level.

All bosses were of the three common types; Awakened Pluto and Hatecraft Hades were observed to take more damage with the same card, though it is not yet known whether or not the ratio remains the same.
