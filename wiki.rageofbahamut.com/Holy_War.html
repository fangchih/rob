Description[edit]

Holy Wars is a large scale Order battle where within a one hour to an hour and a half time frame, order members will engage in battle against each other.

Holy War will trigger when one order declares war, an oppositing order will be choosen randomly and battle notification will be sent to all members in the order.

Players will then engage in combat for Holy War Points and Silver Coins

Roles in Holy War[edit]

Orders are to appoint out the following positions. Lead Positions have special skills that can turn the tides of battle quickly when used properly. With the addition of Position Skills, Teamwork and an organized Order has become extremely essential.

Order Leader[edit]

Order Leaders have an additional Boosts to their ATK and DEF of 5%, grants 1.5 times the amount of Holy War Points you earn from defeating him.

About Positions[edit]

  1. With the addition of Position Skills, the old restriction which prevented you from attacking any opposing Order members until you beat a DEF Leader has been lifted.
  2. The Support Leader Position has been added to Orders.


Position Skill[edit]

  1. Each Position in an Order will have an advantageous Skill in Holy Wars.
  2. You consume SP (Skill Points) when you use these skills.
  3. SP will be raised based on the amount of ATK PWR used at the beginning of the Battle. The amount raised will not be affected by the outcome of the Battle.
    • You can also raise your SP through the 'Charge' button or ally skills.
  4. Depending on your Position, your max SP, SP required to use your Skill and amount recovered via the Charge button will vary.


Position LVL[edit]

  1. Each Position has an associated LVL.
  2. Your Position LVL rises up to 5, based on Holy War Points gained.
  3. Each Position requires a different number of Holy War Points to level-up.
  4. If your Position LVL rises, your Position Skill's effectivesness will also rise.
  5. If you change Positions but then go back to your old Position, you'll start again from your old Position LVL. (e.g. a LVL 2 ATK Leader who becomes a DEF Leader and then ATK Leader will start again at LVL 2.)
  6. Your Position LVL will reset at the end of every Holy Wars event.


How to use skills:[edit]

  • Attack another user to recover SP (SP will recover based on ATK PWR used)
  • Tap the 'Charge' button to recover SP / ATK PWR / DEF PWR (Possible every 20 minutes)
  • Once you have enough SP, tap the launch button to use a skill (Possible whenever SP recovers)



Event Restrictions[edit]

Concerning Positions

  1. During Holy War events, Order Leaders will be able to change other members' Positions.
  2. However, Positions can't be changed between a War Declaration and the 1st battle of a Holy War.


General Event Restrictions During Holy Wars, it will not be possible to:

  1. Join an order
  2. Leave an order
  3. Request to join an order
  4. Cancel your request
  5. Approve/deny order join requests (Order leader only)
  6. Create an order
  7. Disband an order (Order leader only)
  8. Kick members out (Order leader only)
  9. Appoint a new order leader (Order leader only)
  10. Nominate yourself to be order leader
  11. Take over as order leader
  12. Change positions (between a war declarations and the end of that particular holy war).

Critical Hits and Devastating Blows[edit]

Added beginning Holy Wars 11

  • Once in a while, you might deal a critical hit or devastating blow to your opponent.
  • When you deal a critical hit to an opponent, 10% of the holy war points you earn will be taken from your opponent's order. When you deal a devastating blow, this increases to 20%.
  • You must win a battle for a critical hit and devastating blow to affect the holy war points of your opponent's order.
  • If opponent has zero holy war points Critical Hits and Devasting Blows will have NO affect to opponent order's holy war points.

* The normal 5% hit to your opponent's holy war points will not take effect in battles where you deal a critical hit or devastating blow.


Chains[edit]

  1. If you win a Battle and win another within 5 minutes, a Chain will start.
  2. Effects of Chain Bonus:
    • While Chain Bonus is active ATK will be boosted by 10%.
    • The number of the Chain = more Holy War Points (up to a 50% boost).
  3. Holy War Point Bonus from Chain Bonus:
    • If the same Order member adds to the chain, you will get a 1% bonus.
    • If a different Order member adds to the chain, you will get a 2% bonus.
  4. Even if you lose a Battle, the Chain will continue.
  5. The Chain will end if it is not refreshed within 5 minutes.
  6. The Chain can only reach a set number.

Full-force Attacks[edit]

  1. In Holy Wars, you can perform a Full-force Attack by using Holy Powder.
  2. When using Full-force Attack, all of your ATK PWR will be consumed, regardless of the ATK PWR COST of your deck.
  3. You can use either 3 Holy Powders or 1 Holy Powder when you perform a Full-force Attack.
  4. Rules for Full-force Attack:
    • Decks:
      • The deck used will be your selected deck
      • If there is no deck selected, the Recommended ATK Deck will be used
    • Holy War Point Calculation:
      • The amount of Holy War Points accumulated will be based on the full amount of ATK PWR consumed, not the ATK PWR COST of the deck used.
      • If you consume 3 Holy Powders when you battle, you can accumulate points equivalent to the total amount of ATK PWR consumed times 3.
      • You can exceed the Holy War Point limit.
  5. If you lose the Battle, you do not accumulate any Holy War Points.
  6. Only 1 Win or Loss will be counted regardless of how many Holy Powders were used.

If Braveheart is activated by the opponent's Vice-Leader, your Order will not be able to use Full Force Attacks.

Walls[edit]

Walls are built by the Order Leader.
When battling against walls, your attack will be sharply reduced unless you have cards like Master Gunner, Ares, Chaos Valkyrie, Twin Sword or Mythril Golem which increase your damage against walls.

By defeating walls, you earn additional 100 Holy War Points as well.

Twin Sword has the ability to do damage to walls AND the castle.

Holy War Points[edit]

Holy War Points are earned via successful combats in holy war.
Holy War Points are used to determine individual rankings and also for tabulating Order placing.
The more damage you can inflict on an enemy and the higher the challenge awards you more points.
If you were to challenge a higher level player you will earn more points than attacking a lower level player.

New Introduction into Holy Wars 3 is the Point Boosters.
Point Boosters will allow a boost to the total amount of Holy War Points earned after wining a skirmish.
Point Boosters introduced are Sektor, Witch Commander and Principality.
New Point Boosters are Luminous Knight, Chiron and High Werewolf which at their Final Form give 'Great boost'; however, it was only for Holy Wars 8 and 9.


Changes to Holy War Points Received[edit]

Effective as of Holy Wars - Wrath of Lightning

  1. Higher minimum Holy War Points gained when attacking users of a lower LVL.
  2. Higher minimum Holy War Points gained when attacking users with lower DEF PWR.
  3. Different Holy War Point bonus when beating users with Positions:
    • 120% Holy War Points gained for beating the Order Leader; 110% for beating other members with Positions. (Still no bonus for beating regular members)



Ragnarok[edit]

Ragnarok allows attackers a chance to reduce enemies Holy War Points when they win a skirmish.

This will only work when the defender has earned Holy War Points in the current war. If it does not, the enemy Order's Holy War Points will NOT be reduced.
When an attacker successfully defeats a player who has Holy War Points in the skirmish, you will reduce 20% * of that player's current Holy War Points earned thus far.
At the same time the attacker will score more Holy War Points

With recent Holy Wars events, Ragnarok has been active from day 1. This, of course, could change from Holy Wars to Holy Wars.

* As of Holy Wars 11 the following changes are in effect unless otherwise noted:

  • While ragnarok is active, if you win a battle, you'll reduce your opposing order's holy war points by 5% (not 20%) of the holy war points you gain from that battle. However, you will still get 1.5 times the usual holy war points if you win a whole war.


Exploring[edit]

  • You'll be able to explore your surroundings during wars.
  • By using stamina, you'll be able to proceed through different areas which each have various items that are useful in wars.
  • The drop rates of items depend on which area you explore.


Items Found by Exploring

  • Essence of Encouragement: This recovers some of your SP. * If the order leader gets this while shingeki is active, it won't recover their SP.
  • Essence of Loyalty: This recovers some of the order leader's SP. * It wont' recover their SP while shingeki is active.
  • Essence of Enigmas: This recovers some of your ATK PWR.
  • Horn of Warriors: This gives a small boost to holy war points.
  • Horn of Lords: This gives a big boost to holy war points.
  • Horn of the Lion: This gives a great boost to holy war points.
  • Statue of War: This gives all members of your order an ATK boost for a certain period of time. While one is active, getting a second and third one will boost your ATK even further. Getting a fourth one will make the boost last longer.
  • Statue of Destruction: This gives all members of your order an ATK boost against walls/castles for a certain period of time. While one is active, getting a second and third one will boost your ATK even further. Getting a fourth one will make the boost last longer.
  • Blessed Ring: If you have any of this item when you win a battle, one will be used to give a 5% boost to the holy war points that you achieve then. You can have up to 99 at a time.

All Items except for blessed rings will be used as soon as you get them. They cannot be saved in your item list. Blessed Rings do carry over into your next war. </div>

If you have a special Holy Wars card that boosts exploring drop rates, you must add it in the Explore menu in order for its skill to take effect.


Ad Hoc Orders[edit]

  • If you don't belong to an Order by the time the Holy Wars event starts, you'll be added to an Ad Hoc Order automatically. (This is limited to users who meet the requirements to join Ad Hoc Orders.)

What are Ad Hoc Orders?

  • Ad Hoc Orders are formed automatically during Holy Wars event periods.
  • These are formed with users who aren't in Orders.
  • Users under LVL 20 can't join Ad Hoc Orders.
  • Only users who registered their devices can join Ad Hoc Orders.
  • Even if you are invited to a regular Order, an Ad Hoc Order will form first.
  • Once a Holy War event starts, you won't be able to invite others to your Order.
  • If you have an Order you want to join, make sure to do so before Holy Wars starts!
  • Ad Hoc Orders disband when Holy Wars events end.
  • In Ad Hoc Orders there are no Order Leaders or Vice-Leaders. Leaders are assigned automatically to users in the order that they join the Order.
  1. ATK Leader
  2. DEF Leader
  3. onward: Regular Member (not a Leader)
  • Each Ad Hoc Order has a maximum of 25 members.

In Ad Hoc Orders, You Can:

  • Declare war
  • Donate
  • Build Walls
  • Rupies donated or Walls built do not carry over into other Orders.
  • Ad Hoc Order names are created automatically and can't be changed.
  • Please understand that Ad Hoc Order specs may change without prior notice.

Holy War Coins[edit]

You can get a holy war card pack by using either one gold coin or three silver coins.

Getting Gold Coins[edit]

  • You will receive gold coins by getting a set amount of holy war points. The amount of gold coins you receive will increase according to the amount of holy war points you accumulate.
  • If you lose in a holy war you will not receive any gold coins.
  • Listed below are the required amounts of holy war points to receive gold coins:
    • 5,000 points = 1 coin
    • 10,000 points = 2 coins
    • 20,000 points = 3 coins
    • 30,000 points = 4 coins
    • 40,000 points = 5 coins
    • 50,000 points = 6 coins
    • 60,000 points = 7 coins
    • 72,500 points = 8 coins
    • 85,000 points = 9 coins
    • 100,000 points = 10 coins


Getting Silver Coins[edit]

  • You accumulate silver coins by participating in holy wars.
  • Compete within your order to accumulate the most holy war points. You will receive silver coins according to your place with inn the order in one holy war.
  • You cannot share silver coins with players not within your order.
  • You will get 1 silver coin for every 3,000 holy war points you accumulate in 1 Holy War.
  • You'll get up to 300 silver coins from one holy war.

Sharing Silver Coins[edit]

  • By sharing silver coins with an order member, you and the recipient will both receive a silver coin.
  • The amount of silver coins you can share (displayed as "Times Sendable") will be based on the amount of silver coins you have accumulated by participating in a war.
  • Only 1 may be sent to the same order member each day.
  • "Times sendable" will only increase by the number of silver coins you earn from participating in a war.

  • After the event has ended and a certain amount of time has passed, gold coins and silver coins will disappear.
