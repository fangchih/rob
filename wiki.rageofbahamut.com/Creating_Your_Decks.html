Introduction[edit]

You'll find many sites and blogs about which decks to create. Some may be outdated. Here you'll find possible ways to create your decks and reasons behind the 'why?'. Of course, this wouldn't be for the veteran player; however, if you have a tip, please feel free to contribute.


Basics[edit]

You should have few attack decks made and one or two defense decks (although, one generally seems to be more than enough).

For each deck, you will be allowed to add cards up to the total amount of Attack Power (ATK PWR) or Defense Power (DEF PWR) that you've allotted to each attribute. For starters, if you've chosen to go full attack power, then you'll be allowed to build a much stronger deck. Generally, most would start off with DEF PWR at 10 and won't add to it until they've reached level 40 or 50. In this case, there's no defense deck creation until you've allocated enough DEF PWR points. Don't wait too long to raise this attribute! During Holy Wars, attack and defense have become very important with the recent changes in Holy Wars format.


Attack Decks[edit]

List of decks that you should have (given that you have the ATK PWR and cards to do so).

Primary Attack Deck[edit]

This should be your best cards with the best percentage boosts or hits. Although Cygames has stated that skill levels rather than card position would take precedence, I haven't found a major difference. So, this format follows the old rule of card positions with skills secondary.

Cards for our example:

Claymore Maid
High Rare (PWR: 25)
ATK: 3,380
DEF: 2,700
Skill: Big boost to man ATK (13 %)

Master Archer
High Rare (PWR: 25)
ATK: 3,460
DEF: 2,610
Skill: Big boost to ATK (all) (8 %)

Grimoire Mina
SS Rare (PWR: 26)
ATK: 4,970
DEF: 3,410
Skill: Massive hit to foe's DEF (all) (15 %)

Master Thief
High Rare (PWR: 26)
ATK: 3,420
DEF: 2,850
Skill: Big boost to man/gods ATK (10 %)

Endymion
High Rare (PWR: 26)
ATK: 3,120
DEF: 3,150
Skill: Big hit to foe's gods ATK/DEF (8 %)

Phaethon
High Rare (PWR: 26)
ATK: 3,280
DEF: 2,990
Skill: Big boost to man ATK/DEF (8 %)

In first position (will show as center card), this card's skill will always proc (activate). You want your best card based on its percentage boost. So, as an example, if you had a skill 5 Claymore Maid and a skill 10 Master Archer, you'd put Master Archer in first position. Reason? Claymore Maid at skill 5 only gives a boost to your man cards of 17%; whereas, Master Archer at skill 10 would give 18% to all your cards. Now, if Claymore Maid was skill 6, it would be a toss up AND, at skill 7 and above, she would definitely be placed in first position.

In 2nd and 3rd, you can add your next best boost card OR non-specific realm debuff (hit to foe's defense (all)) card. So, with our given cards, Master Archer would be ideal in 2nd and then either Phaethon or Master Thief. Why not Endymion or Grimoire Mina? Because Endymion has a realm specific hit, you would only put him in these slots IF you are creating a realm specific deck (such as Anti-Divine). With Mina, I'd prefer to not have her in my deck considering her average max attack tends to be only 13k or slightly higher (unless you happen to have one of the VERY few cards out there that are 27k). Since this is our Primary Attack Deck, we'll keep her out of these slots for now. Once again, you'd want to put the card with the higher skill percentage next. Let's say our Phaethon is skill 8 (or 15%) and our Master Thief is skill 7 (or 16%). Master Thief would go in slot 3.

In 4th and 5th (a.k.a. the wings), I would put the next set of cards with the highest attack points AND higher skill. Usually for wing cards, you want the higher points to increase your base attack points. Now, assuming all our cards are a 4-7 evolution, I'd put our Phaethon followed by Endymion. Some may choose to put Grimoire Mina since she isn't realm specific. This would be a bad idea in my opinion. As a wing card, you need your base attack to be as high as you can so that your boosts from your other cards actually mean something. By putting in Mina, you're base attack would be short of 3k which sometimes makes a big difference. Ultimately, the choice is up to you, but this is how I would do it with the given cards.

Realm Specific Deck[edit]

With Realm Specific decks, you focus on hitting a particular realm. For our given cards, we would put Endymion in the second position. Why? You want your boosts to go off - always. The card in the second position tends to go off more than any other position and is a safe bet to put a realm specific hit there. If you had Endymion in the first position, you are taking a chance that the current base attack power of that deck will be sufficient enough to defeat your opponent. With Claymore Maid in the first position, you are guaranteed that your cards attack points will be boosted.

After Claymore Maid and Endymion, the following cards would be as usual - cards with the highest skill percentage boost, followed by highest skill percentage hits, and last highest attack points. Follow this formula for a great chance at successful attacks against specific realms. In this case, Gods realm.

If you don't have a card with a skill to do damage against walls or a castle, then skip over this part.

Cards with skills that do damage to walls can be activated up to 3 and they DO stack. Although one is good to have, two would be best. A third seems like over-load given the new rules on multiple cards - unless you happen to use 3 different cards with wall/castle damage skills. In your wings, you should have your highest attack or highest attack boosters.

When creating a Holy War point booster deck, you'll want to only have one of them. Holy War point boosters DO NOT stack - meaning only one will ever activate (unlike wall damage cards).

The most simple way to create this deck is to simply put your Holy War Point Booster card in first position of your primary attack deck. Every card then moves down to the next slot. Reason? You want the boost to Holy War points to activate when using this deck. You would want your boosts to attack to also activate.

If there is a noticeable difference in your base attack with this deck, only use this deck when you know that you can beat your opponent. Basically, rely only on your base attack. Any boost to your attack is icing on the cake.

Now, if a Holy War point booster card is one of your primary cards in your deck, then place this card in the wings.

Scraps[edit]

Many times in Holy Wars you end up with a few ATK PWR left. Most would attack the castle; however, it returns very little Holy War points. Instead, create a 'Scraps' deck. You should already know how many attacks you have per holy powder. Create a deck that cost's only as much as the remaining ATK PWR. Include in that deck (hopefully), your Holy War point booster card and any other boost to attack card that you can. DO NOT GO OVER or else it would be a useless deck to have. Name your deck something that includes the base attack of that deck.

So, my Scraps deck totals 49k with a cost of 54 ATK PWR. I named it "Scraps 49k" so that I know not to hit any opponent stronger than 49k. In most wars, this would be the opponent that your Order constantly attacks (if you view Holy War activity). Preferred method is by communicating with your Order members through a chat app. This will help you maximize your Holy War points AND make good use of your remaining ATK PWR.

Defense Deck[edit]

For your DEF Deck, you will be using many of the same methods you used to create your ATK Deck, but there are some things you may want to consider differently. Such as the fact that you will likely only have one DEF Deck for all realms since you can't predict who will be attacking you and event skills serve no purpose in defense decks.

We'll use the following cards for our example.

Sylvan Wizard
High Rare (PWR: 25)
ATK: 2,900
DEF: 3,180
Skill: Big boost to man ATK/DEF (8 %)

Paladin
High Rare (PWR: 22)
ATK: 2,480
DEF: 2,980
Skill: Big boost to man DEF (13 %)

Guinevere
High Rare (PWR: 18)
ATK: 2,350
DEF: 2,320
Skill: Big boost to DEF (all) (8 %)

Golem Master
High Rare (PWR: 20)
ATK: 2,190
DEF: 2,850
Skill: Big hit to foe's gods ATK (13 %)

Master Summoner
High Rare (PWR: 26)
ATK: 2,820
DEF: 3,450
Skill: Big boost to man/demons DEF (10 %)

Naoise
High Rare (PWR: 26)
ATK: 2,790
DEF: 3,480
Skill: Big hit to foe's ATK (all) (8 %)

The same rules apply for card skills as in your ATK Deck. Position 1 will always activate, so you want your best card skill there. This means you will likely want Paladin for position 1, unless the skill of Master Summoner, Sylvan Wizard or Naoise is high enough to give a larger percentage. Again, the same rules apply for filling out the rest of the deck, with the exception of Golem Master.

The reason Golem Master might not be the best addition to your DEF Deck is it's skill only affects God cards. While in an ATK Deck you have some control over what realm you attack, you don't have that luxury for your defense. If a deck of Man or Demon realm cards attacks you, Golem Master's skill is useless. With that said, you may still put Golem Master in your DEF Deck because his DEF stat is higher than Guinevere's. You would just want Golem Master in the "wings" of your deck instead of the 3 main positions.

Finally, there are some people who don't build a DEF Deck at all. They leave their DEF PWR at 10 and instead put all their points into ATK PWR or Stamina. While this does enable you to get those stats much higher, your defense plays a vital role in some events, it will be more difficult to keep any treasures you find and you will lose rupies with every loss, though the extra attacks you can perform may make up for these drawbacks. It depends entirely on how you want to play the game.

Castle Crushers[edit]

Castle Crushers decks are slightly different. While most of the advice given above still applies, you will need to build 3 decks for ATK and DEF. You cant use the same card twice in the same category either (You can use the same card in both ATK and DEF, but not twice in ATK or twice in DEF.). This doesn't apply to card names, just the card itself, if you have two copies of a card you can use both in one category. These decks also do not use your ATK PWR or DEF PWR to limit what cards you can use, so you can use nothing but your best cards.

If you don't have enough cards, or haven't assigned a card somewhere, any empty spots will be assigned a "Standby Reina" automatically. "Standby Reina" is a card with 100 ATK, 100 DEF, no skill and no realm, so even normal rarity cards are a better choice. Due to it not having a realm, "Standby Reina" will only be affected by card skills that affect all realms.

During play, these decks are chosen randomly throughout Castle Crushers events, with the exception of Untapped Rage (introduced in Castle Crushers 4 - Red Dirge) which will use only your ATK Deck 1. So while it may be advantageous to make your ATK Deck 1 slightly stronger than your other ATK decks, there can't be a large gap in power if you want consistent results.

Castle Crushers score booster cards are applied exactly the same as Holy War score boosters, just make sure they have a Castle Crushers skill, not a Holy War skill. Skills that increase damage to walls don't serve any purpose in Castle Crushers either, so there's no point in adding them into your decks for the skill, but you may need the extra card to avoid having a "Standby Reina" put in you deck.
