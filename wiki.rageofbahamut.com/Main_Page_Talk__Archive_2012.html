Formulas for determining cost effectiveness of Evolved feeders vs Unevolved feeders -- 199.27.128.187 21:26, 22 September 2012 (UTC) -- 199.27.128.187 21:26, 22 September 2012 (UTC) -- 199.27.128.187 21:26, 22 September 2012 (UTC)[edit]

I did my own research and math into figuring out which way to enhance is more cost effective. To simplify the math, I based my formulas off of the percentage of leveling the skills of my fully evolved and max enhanced Sektor. The skill of the Sektor was at level 3.

An unevolved HN card provides 4% chance of skill up. A fully evolved HN card (now a rare) provides 6% chance. All of the percent variables are written as whole numbers. For example, 5% was represented by a 5.

My formulas were:

Unevolved <cost per %> = <Cost of enhancement> / <percentage gained>)

Evolved <Cost Per %> = ( 4 * <cost of single evolution> + <Cost of enhancement> ) / <percentage gained>

in my trial problenm it ended up being:

Cost per % = <Cost of Enhancement> / 4

Cost per % = (1725 + <Cost of enhancement>) / 6


I do not have enough data to further refine this formula (such as how the current skill of the card affects the percentage gained. I tested and noticed that increasing experience yielded a similar percentage difference on experience gained as the chance of skill ups. I entered these formulas into a spreedsheet. My results showed that using unevolved HN feeders are the most cost effective until the enhanced card is level 68. If my math is correct, this is a great starting off point to figure out which way to enhance costs less. you can contact me at winerdarfatherofebis@gmail.com
