Treasure series

  • Every quest can reward you with 3 different cards. Certain quests give treasures as a 4th reward (golden square). When you collect the 6 different colored treasures of one type, you get a treasure card as reward. 6 treasures of one kind are called a treasure series.
  • You can complete a treasures series only once to get the reward.
  • You can still gather the treasures after completing the series to attract people to attack you for honor points. But in this case, you need at least 2 treasures of the same kind and color, to get attacked for it, because the first one will be protected. Once you have completed a set, your last treasure of each color cannot be given away, sold, donated to your Order, or stolen by another player.
  • Every 2nd quest has a treasure of a certain color as reward. After Chapter 34 it is only every 3rd quest.
  • You are not able to obtain every treasure color by questing. You have to battle other players to get the missing colors. Especially at later quest chapters and during quest events, you can only obtain 2 colors. Those are realm specific: man (red, blue), gods (green, yellow), demons (purple, cyan).
  • You can only attack for a treasure, when you obtained already one treasure of the series by questing.
  • You can put a Magic Circle on a treasure to protect it from a single player attack.
  • Treasure cards are not evolveable. But there will be probably a 2nd evolution form in the future. At least Japanese Version has this 2nd form already. As opposed to this Event treasure cards can be evolved to the 4th form.
  • Most treasure cards are pretty much worthless, because they can't be evolved. But people still buy them and use them as skilled feeders.
  • There are quest events where you can get special event treasure series with event cards as reward for a limited time. Example: Tears of the Moon.
  • During quest events you can usually get 3 event treasure series. After completing those, you can get another 3 event treasure+ series, which reward the same cards, but in the 2nd card form (card+).
  • You can gift treasures to other players (doesn't apply to event treasures).
  • You can donate treasures to your Order instead of rupies. Check the treasure information below, to find out how much rupies their donation is worth.
  • When you want to battle for a treasure, you don't have to select "Choose by Treasure Possessed", because this list never shows every player, who actually has the treasure you would like to have. Instead of that, you could select "Choose from Similar LVL", click on an opponent and browse through their treasures, to check whether they have the missing treasure. Another option is to leave one fellow spot open and use the fellow search to find opponents of a certain level and certain realm. That can be very useful for event treasures.
  • You get the red #ring by completing the tutorial (your realm doesn't matter).
