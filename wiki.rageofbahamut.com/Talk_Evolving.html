Percentage Mathematics&lt;editsection-brackets&gt;n">[edit]&lt;editsection-brackets&gt;

Just a little something which could be worth mentioning on this page about how the percentage of stats retained play out. For example, if you start with 2 T1 cards, enhance them and then evolve them together, we know that 10% of it's stats will be retained. This means that we have the bases stats + 10% + 10%. That's where percentage math throws a curve ball. A percentage of a percentage in the vast majority of games is usually a bad deal and RoB is no exception to this rule. This is because this 10% retained stats itself is only represents a fraction of the stats of the new cards and that fraction if multiplied by another fraction. Let's pretend for simplicity sake that that the retained stats represent 10% of the total stats of the new card. If you evolve it again, the stats retained from the T1 card will be... 10% of 10% which really means that only about 1% of the stats gained during the first evolve are retained during the second evolve. The same phenomenon happens during the third evolve.

There's only about a 0.1% gain to be had from evolving a T1 card on the final T4, about 1% from a T2. The T3 gets the full 10% as it's the last in line. Keep in mind, my numbers are not exact, but they do give a good idea of what happens. The stats retained from enhancing a T1 and T2 are questionable at best and only really worth it if you are going for the big one.

Archived Page&lt;editsection-brackets&gt;n">[edit]&lt;editsection-brackets&gt;


== Description ==

Evolution is when you use 2 of the same cards to merge, causing it to evolve to the next form (Up to the 4th stage). <br/>
Evolving will retain 5% or 10% when fully [[maturing|matured]] of the card's stats and add on to the next stage's stats.<br>
If the retained boost results in a floating-point number, the result will be rounded up.<br>
In case of a [[Bunny Lilith Limil]] with 5063 ATK at max level, the exact result should be 506.3,but values will be rounded up to 507.
<br>
<br>
Notes about Evolving to keep in mind:
<br>
If the Base card is considered to be 100%, Base+ is 120%, Base++ is 140%, and Final is 180%
<br>
An example of this is [[Cu Sith]], whose attack stats are: 390, 468, 546, and 702
<br>
Cu Sith is 390<br>
Cu Sith+ is 468, which is 390*1.2, or +20%<br>
Cu Sith++ is 546 which is 390*1.4 or +40%<br>
[Mad Dog] Cu Sith is 702 which is 390*1.8 or +80%<br>

The Japanese Wiki states that there are several common Evolving Patterns to consider<br>
8Full (Requires [[Enhancing]] all 15 stages from tier 1 to the last tiers)<br>
8Straight (Requires Enhancing only the 7 stages from tier 2 to the last tiers)<br>
8-2Before (Requires Enhancing only the 3 stages from tier 3 and the last tiers)<br>
8Normal (Requires Enhancing only at the last tier)<br>
6Full (Requires Enhancing all 11 stages from tier 1 to the last tiers)<br>
6Straight (Requires Enhancing all 7 stages from tier 2 to the last tiers)<br>
6-2Before (Requires Enhancing only the 3 stages from tier 3 and the last tiers)<br>
6Normal (Requires Enhancing only at the last tier)<br>
5Custom (Requires Enhancing all 7 stages from tier 1 to the last tiers)<br>
4Full (Requires Enhancing all 7 stages from tier 1 to the last tiers)<br>
4Custom (Requires Enhancing only the 3 stages from tier 3 and the last tiers)<br>
4Normal (Requires Enhancing only at the last tier)


In terms of final results, in descending order:<br><br>

8-Full > 8-Straight > 6-Full > 6-Straight > 5-Custom > 8-2Before > 6-2Before > 4-Full > 4-Custom > 8-Normal > 6-Normal > 4-Normal<br>
The difference between 8-Full and 6-Straight is 0.52%.<br><br>

8-Full: 100% Efficient<br>
<pre>Enhance everything, throughout all stages
Recommended for your best of the best, only do this if the card needs to be as perfect as can be.

8-Straight: 99.70% Efficient

Don't enhance the first stage, but enhance everything for +, ++, and Final. (Saves Enhancing 8 times, but weaker than 8Full)
Recommended for HR and SR, where you have a decent supply, but want more power without fully committing.

8-2Before: 97.87% Efficient

Don't enhance anything until the 2 ++'s and Final. (Saves a ton on enhancing, but as a result is a lot weaker.)
Recommended for R and HR, where you have a large supply but dont want to waste a ton of resources enhancing.

8-Normal: 87.21% Efficient

Standard 8 card evolve, but only enhancing the final stage. (Only need to enhance 1 thing, but very weak.)
Never do this, unless for N or HN

6-Full: 99.63% Efficient

As with 8Full, enhance everything.(Essentially on par with 8Straight. 2 Cards or 4 max enhances, your choice.  
Probably a good idea if the cards are very expensive but you can enhance them for a cheaper cost.)
Recommended for HR and SR

6-Straight: 99.48% Efficient

Don't enhance 4 of the first stage. The 4 that are used to make the 2 +'s. Then enhance both +'s and both first stages used to make the 2 ++'s.  
Enhance those and then the final for 7 total enhances.(Saves 2 cards and less enhancing but weaker than 6Full)
Recommended for R and HR

5-Custom: 98.43% Efficient

Make a + without enhancing the first stages. Enhance the + and evolve with an enhanced first stage for a ++.
Then make a + by enhancing both first stages. 
Enhance this + and evolve with the enhanced ++ for the final stage for 7 total enhances. (Save 1 card, but weaker than 6Straight)
Recommended for R and HR

Below is the simpler break down of how it is.


=== 4 Card Evolve ===

Base(1) + Base(2) = Base+(1)<br>
Base+(1) + Base(3) = Base++(1)<br>
Base++(1) + Base(4) = Final<br>

=== 5 Card Evolve ===

Base(1) + Base(2) = Base+(1)<br>
Base(3) + Base(4) = Base+(2)<br>
Base+(1) + Base(5) = Base++(1)<br>
Base++(1) + Base+(1) = Final<br>

or<br> (weaker if using Base, instead of Base+, for Final form)

Base(1) + Base(2) = Base+(1)<br>
Base(3) + Base(4) = Base+(2)<br>
Base+(1) + Base+(2) = Base++(1)<br>
Base++(1) + Base(5) = Final<br>

=== 6 Card Evolve ===

Base(1) + Base(2) = Base+(1)<br>
Base+(1) + Base(3) = Base++(1)<br>
Base(4) + Base(5) = Base+(2)<br>
Base+(2) + Base(6) = Base++(2)<br>
Base++(1) + Base++(2) = Final<br>

or<br>

Base(1) + Base(2) = Base+(1)<br>
Base(3) + Base(4) = Base+(2)<br>
Base(5) + Base(6) = Base+(3)<br>
Base+(1) + Base+(2) = Base++(1)<br>
Base++(1) + Base+(3) = Final<br>

=== 7 Card Evolve ===

Base(1) + Base(2) = Base+(1)<br>
Base(3) + Base(4) = Base+(2)<br>
Base(5) + Base(6) = Base+(3)<br>
Base+(1) + Base+(2) = Base++(1)<br>
Base+(3) + Base(7) = Base++(2)<br>
Base++(1) + Base++(2) = Final<br>


=== 8 Card Evolve ===

Base(1) + Base(2) = Base+(1)<br>
Base(3) + Base(4) = Base+(2)<br>
Base(5) + Base(6) = Base+(3)<br>
Base(7) + Base(8) = Base+(4)<br>
Base+(1) + Base+(2) = Base++(1)<br>
Base+(3) + Base+(4) = Base++(2)<br>
Base++(1) + Base++(2) = Final
